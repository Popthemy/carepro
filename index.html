INTRODUCTION

Thank you for using CarePro. This is the page for documentation, support and user manual that will help you to get started and understand all the functionalities of CarePro.

CarePro is a comprehensive end-to-end health information management solution for Hospitals, Clinics and Medical Centers. It connect all health center departments, processes and workflows enabling swift, effective and efficient managemement of health related information and data such as patients records, billing, appointments, consultation, prescription, documents, reports and more.

Functionalities and modules to be discussed in this documentation include patient management, calender management, workbench management, service transactions, billings and payments management, product management, and contact management.

If you already have a CarePro account click here to login. If you are new to CarePro click here to sign up for a free trial, request a quotation or demonstration or request for account setup.

DASHBOARD

The dashboard on CarePro is the page you are redirected to once you successfully login. The page gives you summary of useful metrics to track key performance indicators. These are figures that speaks volume about the critical operation of your medical centre.

Useful Metrics

At the top of the dashboard page, there are some key useful numbers that show the state of your company as a whole. The following key numbers are shown on the dashboard.

  • Patients - Shows the number of patients registered at your medical centre.
  • Visits - Shows the number of visits.
  • Bills - Shows the number of bills.
  • Consult - Shows the number of consultations.
  • Revenue - Show the amount of revenue.

Graphical Data

There are pie charts and bar charts on the dashboard to represent some key performance metrics of your operation.

  • Patients - Pie chart that shows the ratio of male to female patients.
  • Invoices - Pie chart that shows the ratio of paid to unpaid invoices.
  • Departments - Pie chart that shows the departments.
  • Visits - Graph that shows the volume of visits for each day.

    Visits

    This page is used to register patient visits. The steps to register a visit are:

    • Select the visit type
    • Select the department
    • Enter the visit date and time
    • Select the clinician overseeing the visit
    • Attach reference form, if needed
    • Click on 'Submit Only' to proceed without billing the patient or 'Submit and Bill Patient' to register the visit as well as bill the patient

    Billing

    This page is used to bill a patient. The steps to create a new billing are:

    Select the referring entity
    • Select the billing ref, i.e, the patient or some other referring entity
    • Select the referring organisation or click on "Add Organisation" to add a new organisation
    • Select the referring doctor or click on "Add Physician" to add a new physician.

    Click on 'Continue' to bill the patient or organisation

    Add Services

    In this section, add the services to bill the patient. To add a service to bill,:

    • Select the department
    • Select the service name
    • The cost for the service is shown
    • Click on 'Add Service'
    Add Products

    In this section, add the products to bill the patient for. To add a product,:

    • Select the product category
    • Select the drug name
    • The unit cost for the product is shown
    • The available quantity for the product is shown
    • Enter the quantity issued
    • Click on 'Add Product'

    The added services and products are shown, as well as, the invoice total. To submit the bill, click on 'Submit Billing'. You will be asked to confirm the payment

    Payment Confirmation

    To confirm the payment,:

    • Select the payment mode
    • Enter the amount paid
    • Enter the discount amount, if any
    • Select the ledger income account
    • Click on 'Post Payment'

    New Physical Examination

    This is the page to record patient physical examination such as vital signs, skin assessment, head, eye, lung, etc

    To create a physical examination record, provide the following:

    • Examination date
    • Clinician performing the examination

    The following can be recorded:

      Step 1 - Vital Signs
    • Weight(in kg)
    • Height(m)
    • Blood pressure(mmHg)
    • Pulse(bmp)
    • Respiration(bmp)
    • Temperature(deg C)
      • Step 2 - Skin Assessment.

      Choose one of the options

    • New bruises
    • Rashes
    • Swelling
      • Step 3 - Head, eyes, ears, nose and throat assessment

      Assess and enter your findings for the following

    • Head
    • Eyes
    • Ears
    • Smell
    • Throat
      • Step 4 - Neck
    • Assess pain with rotation, jugular venous distention, corrigan's pulse and record observations
      • Step 5 - Lung
    • Patient deeply inhales and hold; observe wheeing and tachypnea
      • Step 6 - Heart
    • Assess pulse; incorporate data from wearables
      • Step 7 - Abdomen
    • Assess if abdomen is firm, tender, or distended
      • Step 8: Extremities
    • Press thumb into pre-tibial area and assess edema perceived temperature
      • Step 9: Neurological
    • Assess speech, gait, Romberg, stand from seated position
      • Step 10: Social Determinant of Health
    • Record diet, physical activity, sleep, stress, housing, transportation, safety, mood, past and current disease, previous operation, family history, etc

    Then, click on 'Submit medication'

    This page is used to view or record patient encounter with a doctor. Click on 'New Consultation' to get started.

    New Consultation

    This is the page to record patient encounter with doctor such as clinical findings, diagnosis, treatment, medication etc

    To create a consultation record, provide the following:

    • Examination date
    • Consultant's name
    • Consultation status

    The following data should be provided:

      Step 1 - Condition
    • Assess and record the patient condition such as diseases, lesions, and disorders
      • Step 2 - Complaints
    • Record all patient complains
      • Step 3: Clinical and Paraclinical findings
    • Record all clinical examination and paraclinical findings
      • Step 4 - Diagnosis
    • - Indentify and record nature of illness or other problem by examination of the symptoms
      • Step 5 - Treatment
    • - Record possible care to improve patient condition as per the nature of illness recorded
      • Step 6 - Prescriptions
    • - Recommend and record drugs and dossages to cure, treat or prevent patient illness as per diagnosis recorded
      • Step 7 - Lab Test / Image Scan
    • Recommed if any lab test, x-ray scan, ct scan, mri scan etc is/are needed
      • Step 8 - Surgery
    • Record if need surgeries to be carried out on the patient
      • Step 9 - Recommendation
    • Record all recommedation giving to the patient
      • Step 10 - Admission

      Indicate if patient need to be admitted into the facility for proper treatment. If yes, provide the:

    • Admission date
    • No. of days
    • Discharge date
